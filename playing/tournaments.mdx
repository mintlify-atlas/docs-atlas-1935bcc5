Lidraughts hosts two tournament formats: **Arena** (continuous-pairing) and **Swiss** (fixed-round). Both formats support all variants, rated play, and team battles.

## Arena tournaments

Arena is Lidraughts' signature tournament format. The event runs for a fixed duration (typically 20 minutes to 6+ hours) and games are paired **continuously** — as soon as you finish one game, you are paired into the next.

### How to join

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a tournament">
    Go to [lidraughts.org/tournament](https://lidraughts.org/tournament). Upcoming and in-progress tournaments are listed on the home page. Featured official tournaments appear on the Lidraughts homepage.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join the tournament">
    Click **Join** on the tournament page. You can join at any point while the tournament is running — even mid-event.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play your games">
    After joining, you will be paired automatically. Play your game and return to the tournament lobby; the next pairing is arranged immediately after your game ends.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Pause or withdraw">
    Click **Withdraw** to temporarily stop being paired. You can rejoin at any time without penalty.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  If you do not make a move in a game, you are removed from the tournament automatically. Reconnect and rejoin to continue participating.
</Tip>

### Arena scoring

Points are awarded per game based on result and streak status:

| Result | Normal | On a streak (2+ consecutive wins) |
|---|---|---|
| Win | 2 points | 4 points |
| Draw | 1 point | 2 points |
| Loss | 0 points | 0 points |

A **streak** starts when you win two games in a row. Each subsequent win while on a streak scores double points. A draw or loss breaks the streak.

### Berserk

Before a game starts, you can click the **berserk** button to halve your clock time in exchange for a bonus point if you win.

- Your clock is cut in half (e.g. 5+0 becomes 2:30+0).
- If you **win** the game with sufficient time used, you earn **+1 bonus point** on top of the normal score.
- If you lose or draw, you gain no bonus — and played with half the time.
- Berserking is only available when the tournament allows it (`berserkable = true`). The tournament organiser can disable berserking.

<Warning>
  A berserk win only counts as a valid berserk (earning the bonus) if the game lasts long enough — very quick wins after berserking may not award the bonus point.
</Warning>

### Arena pairing

Arena uses a **similar-score pairing** algorithm. Players with close scores are paired together, with some randomness to avoid always facing the same opponent. You will not be paired against the same player twice in a row.

## Swiss tournaments

Swiss tournaments are **team-based**, fixed-round events. They must be created within a Lidraughts team (`/swiss/new/:teamId`). The number of rounds is set in advance (default 8, maximum 100).

### How to join

<Steps>
  <Step title="Find a Swiss tournament">
    Swiss tournaments are listed at [lidraughts.org/swiss](https://lidraughts.org/swiss) and on the tournament pages of individual teams.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Join your team's Swiss">
    You must be a member of the team that hosts the Swiss. Click **Join** to register before the event starts.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Play each round">
    Rounds are paired according to the Swiss system. You play one game per round. Results carry forward to determine future pairings.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Wait for next round">
    Between rounds, the system calculates pairings. The round interval is either automatic (based on clock speed) or manually set by the organiser.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Swiss scoring

| Result | Points |
|---|---|
| Win | 1 point |
| Draw | 0.5 points |
| Loss | 0 points |
| Bye (absent) | 0 points |

Tiebreaks use **Sonneborn-Berger** (performance against opponents, weighted by those opponents' scores).

### Swiss settings

| Setting | Default | Range |
|---|---|---|
| Number of rounds | 8 | 3–100 |
| Clock | 3+0 | Any valid clock |
| Rated | Yes | Yes / No |
| Round interval | Automatic | 5 s – 7 days, or manual |

The **automatic round interval** is derived from the clock speed:

| Speed | Auto interval |
|---|---|
| UltraBullet | 5 seconds |
| Bullet | 10 seconds |
| Blitz (< 5 min) | 20 seconds |
| Blitz | 30 seconds |
| Rapid | 60 seconds |
| Classical | 5 minutes |

Organisers can also choose **manual scheduling**, triggering each round themselves via `/swiss/:id/schedule-next-round`.

<Note>
  Swiss tournaments support up to **2,000 players** per event. You can only enter if fewer than half the rounds have been played (`round ≤ nbRounds / 2`).
</Note>

### Exporting Swiss results

Swiss results can be exported in **TRF format** (FIDE standard) via `/swiss/:id.trf`, suitable for importing into tournament management software.

## Tournament types and schedule

Lidraughts runs an automatic schedule of official tournaments covering all variants and speeds.

### Frequency tiers

| Frequency | Description |
|---|---|
| Hourly | Every hour — short, fast events |
| Daily | Once per day — standard community events |
| Eastern | Once per day at a different time (Asia/Oceania-friendly) |
| Weekly | Once per week — longer duration |
| Weekend | Saturday/Sunday special events |
| Monthly | Once per month — prestigious community tournaments |
| Shield | Monthly — prestige title tournaments (see below) |
| Marathon | 24-hour endurance events |
| Yearly | Annual flagship events |

### Duration examples

| Frequency + Speed | Duration |
|---|---|
| Hourly Bullet | 27 minutes |
| Daily Blitz | 120 minutes |
| Weekly Rapid | 4 hours |
| Shield Blitz | 6 hours |
| Marathon | 24 hours |

## Shields

Shield tournaments are **monthly prestige events** for each speed category and each variant. The Shield categories are:

**Speed shields**: Bullet, SuperBlitz, Blitz, Rapid, HyperBullet

**Variant shields**: Frisian, Frysk!, Antidraughts, Breakthrough, Russian, Brazilian

The winner of a Shield tournament **holds the Shield** until the next Shield tournament of the same category. If the current holder plays in the next Shield, they must **defend** the title.

> *"The winner keeps it for one month, then must defend it during the next [variant] Shield tournament!"*

Shield history is available at [lidraughts.org/tournament/shields](https://lidraughts.org/tournament/shields).

## Team Battles

A **Team Battle** is an Arena tournament where players represent their Lidraughts team. The top scores from each team's members are combined to produce a team ranking.

- Up to **100 teams** can participate in a single Team Battle.
- The organiser sets how many **top players per team** count towards the team score (`nbLeaders`, default 5, maximum 20).
- Individual players still score points normally and appear on the individual leaderboard.
- Team standings are updated live during the tournament.

<Info>
  Team Battles can be created from any team page by eligible team leaders. The tournament URL includes `/tournament/team-battle/new/:teamId`.
</Info>

## Leaderboards

Lidraughts maintains arena **leaderboards** tracking the top performers across all official tournaments. The leaderboard is accessible at [lidraughts.org/tournament/leaderboard](https://lidraughts.org/tournament/leaderboard).

## Entry conditions

Tournaments can restrict entry by:

| Condition | Description |
|---|---|
| Minimum rated games | Must have played N rated games in the relevant variant |
| Minimum rating | Must have a rating of at least N |
| Maximum rating | Rating cap (e.g. U1800 tournaments) |
| Titled players only | Must have a verified title |
| Team membership | Team Battles require membership in the relevant team |

<Tip>
  Variant tournaments (Frisian, Antidraughts, etc.) generally have **no entry conditions**, making them open to all players regardless of rating or game count.
</Tip>

## Creating a tournament

Any registered user can create an Arena tournament.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the creation form">
    Go to [lidraughts.org/tournament/new](https://lidraughts.org/tournament/new).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set clock and duration">
    Choose a clock time (0–60 min) and increment (0–60 s). Set the tournament duration (20 min to 6 hours for regular users).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose variant and mode">
    Select any of the seven supported variants and whether the tournament is rated or casual.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Optional settings">
    Optionally add a password (private tournament), enable/disable berserk, enable/disable streaks, add a description, or configure entry conditions.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Publish">
    The tournament enters a waiting period before starting. The default wait is 5 minutes; you can set a custom start date up to 31 days in the future (180 days for team tournaments).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Tournament names cannot contain the word "lidraughts". To use your username as the tournament name, you need at least 10 rated games and an account at least 3 days old, or a verified title.
</Note>
