## Lobby (seek / hook)

The lobby is the central hub for finding opponents. When you post a **hook** (a real-time seek), Lidraughts broadcasts it to all other players in the lobby. Any player whose preferences overlap with yours can click your hook to start the game.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the lobby">
    Navigate to [lidraughts.org](https://lidraughts.org). The lobby shows all active seeks and the current TV game.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure your game">
    Click **Create a game** and set your preferred time control, variant, rating range, and colour preference.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Post the hook">
    Click **OK**. Your seek appears in the lobby grid. When an opponent accepts it, the game starts automatically.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  You can also click an existing hook in the lobby to accept someone else's seek instantly.
</Tip>

The lobby setup is handled by the `/setup/hook` endpoint. Hooks are **real-time only**; correspondence seeks are posted as named **seeks** visible to logged-in users.

## Challenge a friend

You can send a direct challenge to any Lidraughts user, bypassing the lobby entirely.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the friend challenge form">
    Go to [lidraughts.org/setup/friend](https://lidraughts.org/setup/friend), or click a player's name and choose **Challenge to a game**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set game options">
    Choose variant, time control, mode (rated/casual), and your colour preference. Friend challenges also support the **micro-match** option (a two-game mini-match with reversed colours).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Send the challenge">
    The opponent receives a notification and can accept or decline from [lidraughts.org/challenge](https://lidraughts.org/challenge).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Friend challenges can be **casual or rated**, and support every variant available on Lidraughts. Correspondence (days-per-move) challenges are also supported.
</Note>

## Play vs Computer

Lidraughts integrates [Scan 3.1](https://github.com/rhalbersma/scan) — Fabien Letouzey's engine for 10×10 draughts variants. Computer games are always **casual** (unrated) and are set up at `/setup/ai`.

### Difficulty levels

The AI has **8 levels**, from 1 (beginner) to 8 (strongest). The default is level 1.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Beginner (1–2)">
    Makes deliberate mistakes and plays slowly. Suitable for beginners learning piece movement and basic captures.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Intermediate (3–5)">
    Plays reasonably solid draughts with occasional inaccuracies. Good for practising tactics.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Advanced (6–8)">
    Plays at a strong club-player level and above. Level 8 represents near-engine strength.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

### Supported variants

The computer can play **all 10×10 variants** (Standard, Frisian, Frysk!, Antidraughts, Breakthrough) as well as **From Position**. The 8×8 variants (Russian, Brazilian) are available in human-vs-human games but are not listed among the AI variants in the current configuration.

<Info>
  You can also set a custom starting position (FEN) when playing against the computer by selecting **From Position** as the variant.
</Info>

## Correspondence

Correspondence games use a **days-per-move** clock instead of a countdown timer. Each player has a fixed number of days to make each individual move.

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| Time mode | Correspondence |
| Days per move | 1–14 days (configurable) |
| Default | 2 days per move |
| Rated | Yes (when between registered users) |

<Note>
  Correspondence seeks are only available to **logged-in users**. Anonymous players cannot post or accept correspondence seeks.
</Note>

Correspondence games are a good way to study positions deeply — you can use the board analysis tool between moves.

## Time controls

Lidraughts classifies real-time games by the estimated total duration of a game. The categories and their clock ranges are:

| Category | Estimated duration | Example clocks |
|---|---|---|
| UltraBullet | < 30 s | 0+1, 15+0 |
| HyperBullet | 30 s – 1 min | 0+2, 30+0 |
| Bullet | 1 – 2 min | 1+0, 1+1, 2+0 |
| Blitz | 2 – 8 min | 3+0, 3+2, 5+0 |
| Rapid | 8 – 25 min | 10+0, 10+5, 15+0 |
| Classical | ≥ 25 min | 20+10, 30+0 |

The speed category determines which rating you use for a game. Each variant has its own rating for each speed category.

<Tip>
  Clock times range from 0 to 180 minutes; increments range from 0 to 180 seconds. A game with no time and no increment is played as **Unlimited** (untimed).
</Tip>

## Time mode summary

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Real-time">
    Both players have a countdown clock. This is the standard mode for lobby seeks and tournaments.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Correspondence">
    Each player has a set number of days per move. Games can span days or weeks.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Unlimited">
    No clock at all. Always casual. Useful for analysis or leisurely play.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>
