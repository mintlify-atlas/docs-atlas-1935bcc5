Lidraughts supports **seven draughts variants** across two board sizes. Five variants are played on the 10×10 international board; two are played on the 8×8 board.

## Variant overview

| Variant | Board | Starting pieces | Win condition | Key rule difference |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Standard | 10×10 | 20 vs 20 | Capture or block all opponent pieces | International FMJD rules |
| Frisian | 10×10 | 20 vs 20 | Capture or block all opponent pieces | Pieces capture horizontally & vertically too |
| Frysk! | 10×10 | 5 vs 5 | Capture or block all opponent pieces | Frisian rules, starts with 5 pieces on the back rank |
| Antidraughts | 10×10 | 20 vs 20 | Lose all your pieces (or be blocked) | Goal is reversed |
| Breakthrough | 10×10 | 20 vs 20 | First to promote a piece to king | No capture objective |
| Russian | 8×8 | 12 vs 12 | Capture or block all opponent pieces | Free capture choice; promotion mid-capture |
| Brazilian | 8×8 | 12 vs 12 | Capture or block all opponent pieces | International rules on an 8×8 board |

## Standard

**International draughts** (FMJD rules) on the classic 10×10 board. This is the most widely played form of draughts in the world and the default variant on Lidraughts.

> *"Standard rules of international draughts (FMJD)."*

### Rules summary

- Pieces move **diagonally forward** one square.
- Captures are **mandatory** and must take the maximum number of pieces.
- A piece reaching the back rank becomes a **king**, which can move and capture any distance along diagonals.
- The game is drawn after 25 consecutive king moves without capture, or by threefold repetition, or under specific endgame material rules (e.g. 5-move rule, 16-move rule).

<Note>
  Standard is governed by the official **FMJD (Fédération Mondiale du Jeu de Dames)** rules. See the official rules in `/doc/variant/standard/`.
</Note>

### Draw rules (key material endings)

| Situation | Moves to draw |
|---|---|
| Single king vs. king + ≤2 pieces | 5 moves each |
| Single king vs. king + 3 pieces | 16 moves each |
| 25 consecutive king moves | Automatic draw |
| Threefold repetition | Automatic draw |

## Frisian

Frisian draughts plays exactly like Standard but adds **horizontal and vertical captures** for both pieces and kings.

> *"Pieces can also capture horizontally and vertically."*

### Key differences from Standard

- Capture directions: diagonals **plus** up, down, left, right — eight total directions.
- Capture selection uses **value priority**: a king capture (value 199) must be preferred over a man capture (value 100) when both are possible with equal count.
- **Draw rules**: when only kings remain, the game draws after 7 moves each if one side has two kings vs one, or after 2 moves each when both sides have a single king.

<Info>
  Frisian draughts originates from the province of Friesland in the Netherlands. The official rules are provided as a PDF in the repository at `doc/variant/frisian/OfficialRulesFrisianDraughts.pdf`.
</Info>

## Frysk!

Frysk! is **Frisian draughts starting with 5 pieces each**, placed on the back rank.

> *"Frisian draughts starting with 5 pieces each."*

### Starting position

- **White**: squares 46–50 (back rank)
- **Black**: squares 1–5 (back rank)
- FEN: `W:W46,47,48,49,50:B1,2,3,4,5:H0:F1`

All Frisian capture and movement rules apply unchanged. With only 5 pieces on each side, the opening phase is extremely tactical and games are typically short and sharp.

<Tip>
  Because all pieces start as men on the promotion rank, the very first move of the game can produce a king if captures are forced.
</Tip>

## Antidraughts

The goal in Antidraughts is the **opposite** of Standard draughts: you must lose all your pieces, or be left with no legal moves.

> *"Lose all your pieces (or run out of moves) to win the game."*

### Rules summary

- All Standard movement and capture rules apply (captures are still mandatory).
- The player who **runs out of moves** (all pieces captured or all pieces blocked) **wins**.
- Threefold repetition still applies.
- There are **no draw rules** based on material or king moves.

<Warning>
  Mandatory captures still apply in Antidraughts. You cannot voluntarily skip a capture — even if it means your piece will be taken next move.
</Warning>

## Breakthrough

In Breakthrough, the first player to **promote a piece to king wins** immediately.

> *"The first player who makes a king wins."*

### Rules summary

- All Standard movement rules apply (pieces move diagonally forward).
- Captures are mandatory but do **not** win the game on their own.
- The game ends **immediately** when either player promotes a piece to king.
- If a player is stalemated (no moves), the **opponent wins**.
- There are **no draw rules** — the game always ends with a winner.

<Note>
  Breakthrough is the most forward-oriented variant on Lidraughts. Deep calculation of breakthrough paths is the key skill.
</Note>

## Russian

Russian draughts on an **8×8 board** with several rule differences from Standard.

> *"Choice of capture, promotion during a multi-capture."*

### Key rules

- Board: **8×8**, with 12 pieces per side in three ranks.
- Pieces move forward diagonally; kings move **any distance** along a diagonal (long-range kings, same as Standard).
- **Free capture choice**: you are not required to take the maximum number of pieces — any legal capture sequence is valid.
- **Promotion mid-capture**: a man that reaches the promotion rank during a multi-capture sequence promotes to king and can immediately continue capturing as a king.
- Governed by **FMJD64 (Russian Federation) rules**.

### Draw rules (Russian-specific)

| Rule | Condition |
|---|---|
| 7.2.3 | Threefold repetition |
| 7.2.4 | 3+ kings vs 1 king — draw if not captured within 15 moves |
| 7.2.5 | Only king moves (no captures, no man moves) for 15 moves |
| 7.2.6 | 4–5 piece endings: draw in 30 moves; 6–7 piece endings: draw in 60 moves |
| 7.2.7 | 3-piece strong side vs lone king on long diagonal: draw in 5 moves |
| 7.2.8 | 2-piece strong side vs lone king: draw in 5 moves |

<Info>
  Russian draughts supports **opening tables** from both the FMJD and IDF (International Draughts Federation) databases, enabling thematic tournament positions.
</Info>

## Brazilian

Brazilian draughts uses the **same rules as international (Standard) draughts** but is played on an **8×8 board** with 12 pieces per side.

> *"Same rules as international draughts, played on an 8x8 board."*

### Key rules

- Board: **8×8**, with 12 pieces per side (same starting position as Russian).
- **Maximum capture rule**: you must always take the maximum possible number of pieces (unlike Russian).
- Kings are long-range (move any distance diagonally).
- Draw rules follow the same Russian draw rules (7.2.x series).
- **No promotion mid-capture**: a man that reaches the promotion rank ends its capture sequence (unlike Russian).

<Note>
  The key distinction between Russian and Brazilian is the capture rule: Brazilian requires **maximum captures** (like Standard/International), while Russian allows free choice.
</Note>

## Choosing a variant

<Accordion title="Which variant should I start with?">
  **Standard** is the best entry point. It is the most documented, has the largest player pool, and all other 10×10 variants are extensions of its rules. The FMJD rules for Standard are internationally recognised and well-documented.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="What is the difference between Russian and Brazilian?">
  Both are played on an 8×8 board with 12 pieces per side. The key difference is the capture rule: **Russian** lets you choose any legal capture sequence (free choice), while **Brazilian** requires you to capture the maximum number of pieces (like international Standard rules).
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Are there puzzles for every variant?">
  Yes — Lidraughts has a dedicated puzzle set for Standard, Frisian, and Russian. See the [Tactics puzzles](/playing/puzzles) page for details.
</Accordion>

<Accordion title="Can I play all variants against the computer?">
  The Scan 3.1 engine supports all five 10×10 variants (Standard, Frisian, Frysk!, Antidraughts, Breakthrough). The 8×8 variants (Russian, Brazilian) are currently human-vs-human only in AI games.
</Accordion>
